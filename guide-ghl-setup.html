GHL Setup - The Full Build

Your CRM is the engine room: pipeline, calendar, lead forms, workflows. Here is the whole build, step by step - the same order we run in the room.

6 stepsDay 1 morningWe do it together

💡 Why this order matters

Everything downstream - your ads, your AI receptionist, your follow-up - reads from GHL. Pipeline first so leads have somewhere to live, calendar so they can book, forms so ads can capture, workflows so nothing leaks. Then we TEST before any ad spend touches it.

1

Basic setup

Load in your logo, business name, address and email. Connect your social accounts (Facebook + Instagram). Set your email reply address so responses come back to you.

Settings → Business Profile
2

Pipeline + opportunities + SMS registration

Your pipeline is the money view: every lead becomes an opportunity card that moves across stages, so you can see exactly where every potential patient sits and what they are worth. We build these stages:

New Lead → 1st-5th Follow-Up → Follow Up Later (adds a task) → Booked/Won → Lost/Cancelled

Automations wired here: the Won tag triggers the Booked flow, and DND switches on so booked patients stop getting chased.

SMS registration: Australia requires sender registration. Bring your business registration PDF (free from ABN Lookup) - we submit the SMS certificate application together on the day. Approval takes days, which is why this happens on Day 1, not after.
3

Calendar - two options

Option A - go native (recommended): your rooms, staff and services live in GHL and everything books there. One source of truth, nothing to sync.

Option B - new GHL calendar from our snapshot: one click loads our pre-built calendar setup into your account:

Load the calendar snapshot →
Keeping Timely/Cliniko/Fresha? Then the calendars must agree in BOTH directions or you will double-book: GHL bookings copy across to your old tool, and bookings landing in your old tool (from ads or phone) must block GHL. That is the calendar bridge - Zapier does the reverse lane (Timely "New Booking" → filter out bookings marked "Mirrored from GHL" → create the GHL contact + appointment). Read: do you need this?
4

Lead forms

Two forms: a treatment-specific lead form (this is what your Meta ads connect to) and a website lead form for your site. The treatment form asks just enough to qualify - name, contact, concern - because every extra field costs you leads.

Sites → Forms → Builder
5

Workflows - the four that run everything

New Lead Workflow - instant reply the second a form is submitted (speed = conversion).
Internal Notification Workflow - your team gets pinged so a human can jump in fast.
Pipeline Movement Workflow - opportunity cards move stages automatically as things happen.
Enable DND Workflow - booked patients stop receiving chase messages.

Where Zapier fits with ads: Meta lead forms connect to GHL natively (Integrations → Facebook) - no Zapier needed there. Zapier earns its keep on the calendar reverse-lane above and the odd tool with no direct connection.
6

TEST. Before any ad spend.

Submit your own lead form and watch the whole chain fire: opportunity card appears in the pipeline, internal notification lands, auto-reply goes out. If any link in that chain is silent, we fix it NOW - not after ads are live.

FB Lead Form Test Tool →
