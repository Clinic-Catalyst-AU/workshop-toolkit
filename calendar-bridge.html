Before you decide

Your booking calendar, one source of truth

A five-minute read that could save you a subscription and a headache. Whether you need a calendar bridge at all, and what it really costs if you keep your old tool.

The principle

Everything books in one place

GHL is your source of truth. You build your rooms, machines, services and staff inside GHL once, your clients book there, and your whole system - reminders, follow-up, the AI receptionist, reporting - runs off that one calendar.

When everything lives in one place, there is nothing to keep in sync. No bridge, no second login, no extra monthly fee, no moving part that can quietly break. This is the sturdiest setup, and it is the one we want for you.

When you might want a bridge

Only if you keep your old tool

If you are not ready to let go of your old booking tool (Timely, Cliniko, Fresha) and want to run it alongside GHL for a while, the two calendars have to agree - or you will double-book.

A calendar bridge is a small agent that watches GHL and copies every confirmed booking across into your old tool automatically, so they stay matched. That is the only reason to build one. It is a comfort blanket for the transition, not an upgrade.

How hard it is depends on your tool

Two very different situations

✅ Smooth

Your tool gives us a key (Timely, Cliniko)

These let our agent talk to them directly and cleanly. The bridge runs in the cloud, 24/7, with your laptop closed. You would barely know it is there.

⚠ Awkward

Your tool will not give us a key (Fresha)

With no key, we fall back through three options, in order, and take the first that works:

  1. Connect it through Zapier? Some tools allow this - but it is another paid subscription sitting in the middle of your bookings.
  2. Any hidden way in? Occasionally there is one.
  3. Drive it by hand. The agent opens your booking tool in a browser and books each appointment the way a person would. Fresha lands here.

⚠ That last option is fragile by nature

  • It only runs while your computer is on.
  • The login can time out - and logging back in can ask for a code.
  • If your booking tool changes its screens, it can quietly stop working until we fix it.

Your bookings are never lost - they wait in line and catch up the moment things are healthy again - but it needs babysitting, where going native in GHL needs none.

🎯

Our honest recommendation: go native

Build your services in GHL, take your bookings there, and skip the bridge entirely. It is cheaper, sturdier, and genuinely hands-off.

But this is your call, and we will not leave you stuck. If you want to keep your old tool for now, we build the bridge with you, live, in the room - the one piece we set up together on the day, because it needs your own booking details. You walk out with it working, knowing exactly what it does and what it costs you to keep it.
